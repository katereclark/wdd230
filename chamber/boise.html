Boise, Idaho

History

Boise is known as the "City of Trees" and was orginally named "Les Bois", meaning woods, by French-Canadians in the 1830s. Around the 1860s, gold deposits brought many people to the area and many farms and communities sprung up as a result. Residing in Ada County, Boise is now the capital of Idaho and is one of the largest cities in the state.

Demographics

Today, Boise is a rapidly growing city with a population of 229,993 in 2021. It is also the 15th fastest growing city in the United States and has had an annual growth rate of about 2.5% in recent years. There are slightly more females than males and the median age of people living in Boise is thirty-six years-old.

The average income for many households in Boise is often high due to the large number of jobs available in a variety of different industries. The average family size is about three.
